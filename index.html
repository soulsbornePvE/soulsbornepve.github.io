The Honest Summary

This build wins in PvE for one reason above all others: the Whirligig Saw's transformed spin deals ~2,400 damage per second when you have an opening, compared to ~660 DPS for most other weapons on their best attacks. When bosses commit to long animations, the spin ends the fight.

When bosses are aggressive and you can only land one hit before dodging, the Saw Spear's fast R1 and quick recovery is the right tool. The two weapons together cover every situation in the game.

The Chikage (Bloodtinge build) has higher single-hit AR on paper, but it drains your HP in transformed mode, has poor rally, and requires 50 Bloodtinge that helps nothing else. In long PvE boss fights, that's a losing trade. The Whirligig wins on sustained encounters and the Saw Spear covers aggressive ones.

Start with the Saw Cleaver (starting weapon) — it deals 20% bonus damage vs beasts in transformed mode, which helps against early beast-type enemies and the first boss.

Pick up the Saw Spear in Central Yharnam as soon as possible. It becomes your main weapon through the entire early and mid game.

It is also available to buy from the Bath Messenger in Hunter's Dream after getting the Saw Hunter Badge — found in those same sewers on a corpse near the gate.

3
Whirligig Saw — The Main Event
Old Hunters DLC — earliest access after Vicar Amelia

The best PvE weapon in the game. The transformed spin deals continuous blunt damage at ~3.5 hits per second, staggers most enemies through their attacks, and each tick restores rally HP. Nothing else compares for sustained damage during boss openings.

How to Access the DLC Defeat Vicar Amelia in Cathedral Ward. Then go to Hunter's Dream — a messenger bath near the stairs will have the Eye of a Blood-drunk Hunter item. Pick it up and use it from your inventory. You'll be transported to the Hunter's Nightmare lamp. This is the DLC entrance.
Exact Location — Whirligig Saw From the Hunter's Nightmare lamp, proceed into the area through the river of blood section and up into a large building. The Whirligig Saw is on a corpse on a ledge in the early section of Hunter's Nightmare — before the first mini-boss encounter. You cannot realistically miss it by exploring normally. Requires 18 STR / 12 SKL minimum.

How to Use It

  • Normal R1: Fast physical/blunt hits for quick enemies or tight spaces
  • Transformed spin (hold L2): Continuous blunt damage. This is your primary tool on bosses with openings. Drains stamina — release when low, recover, spin again.
  • Transformed charged R2: Massive single hit after a stagger or parry visceral. 1.5–2.0× multiplier.
  • Spin uses stamina, NOT bullets

When NOT to Spin

  • Aggressive bosses with tiny windows — use normal R1 or swap to Saw Spear
  • Tight indoor spaces — the spin can clip on walls and deal no damage
  • Enemies that require precise targeting — the spin hits everything around you
  • When you only have stamina for one hit — a single normal R1 is more reliable
No Uncanny / Lost Version Needed Unlike the Saw Spear, there is no widely documented Uncanny or Lost Whirligig Saw in community glyph databases. The standard version is what you use. Its Radial · Triangle · Waning slots work fine — fill Radial and Triangle with your best gems, and put a Droplet or Waning gem in the last slot.

Why 50 STR is the Priority

The Whirligig Saw has a Strength coefficient of 1.10 — the highest of any weapon in the game, tied only with Logarius' Wheel and the Chikage's Bloodtinge. Every point of STR contributes 10% more damage than on a weapon with a 1.0 coefficient. At 50 STR the attribute rating is 0.85, meaning:

Whirligig Phys AR = 190 × (1 + 0.85 × 1.10 + 0.50 × 0.30) = 190 × 2.085 = ~396 base before gems

With 3× Adept Blunt gems (32.6% each = 97.8% bonus): 396 × 1.978 = ~783 AR on the spin

Chalice Dungeon Glyphs

What is a Glyph?

A glyph is a code you type into the game that loads another player's pre-made Chalice Dungeon — including their layout, their loot, and their boss drops. This is how the community farms specific gems, weapons, and runes without grinding randomly for hundreds of hours.

How to Use a Glyph — Step by Step

  1. Go to the Ritual Altar in Hunter's Dream — the large stone altar at the back of the dream near the gravestones.
  2. Make sure you have the right Root Chalice — each glyph requires a specific type. See the table below. Root Chalices are the reusable ones (e.g. "Pthumeru Root Chalice").
  3. Interact with the altar and select the matching chalice type. Then choose "Search by Chalice Dungeon Glyph".
  4. Type the glyph code exactly as listed. Not case-sensitive.
  5. If the glyph requires F/R/C — purchase Fetid, Rotted, and/or Cursed Offerings from the Bath Messenger and use them when creating the dungeon.
  6. Enter the dungeon from the lamp near the altar. Navigate to the listed location and collect your item or fight the boss for gems.
Requirements PSN connection required — glyphs are online only. Servers are still active as of 2026. You need the matching Root Chalice unlocked first.

What Are "Layers" and "Bonus Rooms"?

Chalice dungeons have multiple Layers (floors), each ending in a boss. Within each layer there are two bonus room types beyond the main path:

  • Pre-lamp bonus room: A room before the layer's lamp (fast travel point). Requires finding and pulling a lever.
  • Pre-boss bonus room: A room near the boss fog gate. Usually easier to find.

Blood Gems — Best Setup

Understanding Gem Slots

Weapons have up to 3 gem slots — Radial, Triangle, Waning, or Circle. Gems must match the slot shape. Droplet gems fit any slot. The goal is to get the right slot configuration by choosing the right weapon variant (Normal, Uncanny, or Lost).

The Uncanny Saw Spear (glyph 7hqg2h9a) has Radial · Radial · Triangle — perfect for physical builds. The standard Whirligig Saw has Radial · Triangle · Waning — fill the Waning with a Droplet or whatever you have.

Endgame Gem Farm — Step by Step

For the Merciless Watchers farm (best gems in the game):

  1. Unlock Pthumeru Ihyll Root Chalice — requires completing all Pthumeru dungeons through the Defiled Chalice (the one that cuts your HP by 50%). This is the hardest gate in the game.
  2. Enter glyph zkkuat7j — F/R/C Pthumeru Ihyll Root required. Layer 1 Merciless Watchers boss drops 27.2% Tempering Radial and 32.6% Adept Radial gems.
  3. Enter glyph zbc6bqes — F/R/C Isz Root required. Same boss type drops the Triangle version.
  4. Equip Eye runes (+100 Discovery) to increase gem drop rates.
  5. Kill the boss, check drops, quit to menu, re-enter. Repeat until you have 3 gems with acceptable secondary effects. Cursed secondaries are fine in PvE.

The Key Insight — Why Per-Hit AR Isn't Everything

The Chikage and Ludwig's Holy Blade deal more damage per single hit. But "best PvE build" is not the same as "highest single hit." What matters is total damage dealt in a boss fight, and that depends on:

  • How many hits you can land per second — the Whirligig spin lands 3.5 hits/second vs ~0.9 for a greatsword R1 chain
  • Whether you stay alive long enough — the Chikage drains your own HP in transformed mode, Chikage has poor rally, and the Whirligig's spin restores rally HP on every tick
  • Whether the weapon works on every enemy — physical Blunt works on everything; elemental builds require switching and setup

When You Can Only Land One Hit Before Dodging

You correctly identified this — many bosses (Orphan of Kos, Lady Maria, Gascoigne) give tiny windows. In those situations, single-hit damage matters more and the Whirligig's advantage narrows. The honest answer for those fights:

  • Use Saw Spear R1 — faster recovery than Whirligig, lets you dodge sooner
  • Don't try to spin — the spin is a reward for reading the boss, not a default
  • The Whirligig still isn't wrong — its single R1 damage is competitive, and when an opening appears you can briefly spin before dodging

Why Not Arcane?

Arcane is situationally stronger — the right element on the right boss can deal roughly double damage. Rom with a Tonitrus (bolt, DEF 45) deals far more per hit than the Whirligig. But:

  • You need to carry multiple weapons and switch between fights
  • If you pick the wrong element or the boss resists it, you're doing less damage than physical
  • The spin's DPS advantage compensates for neutral elemental matchups on most bosses
  • Physical Strength builds work against everything with zero preparation

Arcane is the best second playthrough build when you know every boss's weakness in advance. For a first playthrough, Strength/Whirligig is the right answer.

Why Not Chikage/Bloodtinge?

The Chikage has higher single-hit AR on paper and scored best in the raw damage analysis. But in a real PvE boss fight:

  • It drains your HP in transformed mode — the mode where all the damage comes from. In a 2-minute boss fight you can lose 30–40% of your HP to your own weapon.
  • Poor rally — you can't recover the HP your weapon drains by hitting the boss. The Whirligig spin restores rally on every tick.
  • 50 Bloodtinge helps nothing else — every other build's primary stat buffs multiple weapons. Bloodtinge is almost exclusively for the Chikage and Bloodletter.
  • Blood damage is often resisted — many bosses have higher blood defense than physical defense.

High damage output that kills you in the process is not a good PvE build. The Whirligig wins on total net damage output while staying alive.

Final Verdict The Strength/Whirligig Saw build wins in PvE because it combines very high sustained DPS, the best stagger in the game, the best rally in the game, strong survivability, and works on everything without preparation. No other build scores highly across all five categories simultaneously. Use the Saw Spear alongside it for aggressive bosses and specific elemental situations — together they cover every encounter in the game.
